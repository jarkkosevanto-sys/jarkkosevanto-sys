20+ years in payments infrastructure. VP roles at Visa across Visa Direct, tokenization, and card platforms. The kind of work that lives underneath every tap, transfer, and push notification — network rules, settlement rails, approval rate optimization, partner integrations across the US, Europe, and Asia Pacific.

These days I'm also building things, which is new territory for me in the best possible way. Well, it's not entirely new but it's been a long time since I was building or writing code...

My original coding experience is gcc, make, and micro-emacs writing C on university workstations. Then a long gap where I was a product person who could read code but I didn't really need to write it. What's happened in the last couple of years with AI-assisted development has genuinely changed that equation — I can now take an idea from concept to working prototype in an afternoon, which has changed how I think about product work. Prototypes are faster to argue with than PRDs. A working demo closes more alignment gaps than a slide deck.

The repos here are a mix of payments domain exploration and personal projects built for fun. All of them were built with heavy AI assistance, which is the point.

---

## What's here

**[payment-router-demo](https://github.com/jarkkosevanto-sys/payment-router-demo)** — An interactive prototype exploring AI-driven routing across cross-border payment rails (VisaNet, VPL, SWIFT, stablecoin). Models corridor-specific constraints, FX costs, settlement SLAs, and regulatory flags per route. Built to make the routing logic tangible, not just describable.

**[Lightspark-Grid-API-demo](https://github.com/jarkkosevanto-sys/Lightspark-Grid-API-demo)** — Hands-on exploration of the Lightspark Grid API in sandbox. Two demos: a payment lifecycle walkthrough and a routing comparison. Built to understand what the developer integration experience actually feels like from the outside.

**[kitewind](https://github.com/jarkkosevanto-sys/kitewind) / [kitewind-server](https://github.com/jarkkosevanto-sys/kitewind-server)** — A personal iOS app for tracking wind forecasts at kite spots and pushing notifications when conditions cross a threshold. Built with Expo/React Native and a Node.js backend. Pure hobby project — also the one that made it obvious how far the tooling has come.
